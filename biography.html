Introduction

Hello everyone! My name is Owen, and I was born in Shanghai, China -- one of the busiest, prosperious metropolises. I moved to Canada in grade 9. As a newcomer, I struggled to make progress in English. However, I always belived in being optimistic as it could help me face the challenges positvely. That made me brave to speak English with my friends, and took the advantage of the environment I was living in to maximally elevate my English skills. Meanwhile, I found my career interest through trial-and-error in learning new skills across hundreds of fields. By spending time probing for different fields of study, I became aware of the growth of technology industry. I therefore challenged myself by taking Mosh Hamedani's Ultimate Java series. After I completed a third of it, I yelled to my mom, "This is what I want to do in my future!" My mom also supported my choice as she often saw me sitting in front of a computer and writing code persistently for 3 hours. -- "Writing a program that simulates a real world problem has so much fun!" Undaunted by the magic of seeing problems being solved in real time, I persisted in acquiring new coding knowldge from Mosh's tutorials daily and had taken part in coding competitions held by Waterloo (Canadian Computing Competition) in which I scored decent marks.

My Reading Recommendation

Here is where I recommand books that have changed my whole life and paradigms.

Fahrenheit 451 The Kite Runner Atomic Habits 1984

Book 1: Fahrenheit 451

Fahrenheit 451 is one of the most inspiring books I have ever read in my life, and it triggered my passion for reading. The book is based on a simulated world in which technology dominates; people always use their technology negatively by neglecting face-to-face conversation, sports and social lives. Also, it captures a society in which books are strictly banned under censorship. If you collect books at home, the police can give you the death penalty, because they think you have an unchaste, distorted mind that can be harmful to the technology-disoriented society. Interestingly, with the alignment of the police's job, firemen are asked to burn the books rather than to put out fires. Overall, this dystopian book has been a great inspiration to lots of young readers, since the story is highly related to our day-to-day life and it seems likely to happen in our lives as the technology industry is skyrocketing. It gives everyone a warning to not fully depend on technology, because it would eventually lead to the tragic ending described in the book.

Book 2: The Kite Runner

I enjoyed reading the Kite Runner. This is one of the best non-fiction books I have ever read. The story reflects the cruelty of the war in Afghanistan's history. However, author Hosseni chose to focus more on a family's relationship, which brought me into tears many times. There are many heartbreaking and heartwarming scenes that have been foreshadowed and somewhat predictable. One of the most valuable things to me by reading this novel is that I developed my ability to imagine while reading, which increased my interest in reading as well. I strongly recommend this book to high school students, because the story was vividly depicted and could trigger their nerve to read more non-fictions. Once you start reading it, you can hardly put it down.

Book 3: Atomic Habits

Atomic Habits -- An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones. As its name, this book can build awareness of one's good and bad habits by applying effective implementations to strength the good ones and break the bad ones. The book could have taken me a month to borrow from the library because of the long waiting list. However, I finally decided to buy one from a local bookstore. Honestly, this is the book that shaped my life the most; I felt the power of a fiction book. This book is structured clearly with visual graphs every five to ten pages and a little summary near the end of each chapter. The author discussed four core concepts of building and destorying every habit -- cue, craving, respose, and reward. While reading the book, I also tried to discover my bad habits and improve. Surprisingly, by applying these principles, I was able to detect my bad habits in my life and to change them in an astonishing speed. For example, I used to use my bathrobe to clean my mouth after brushing my teeth because it was close to me. My mom was always mad at me because of this bad habit. By applying the principles in this book, I completely got rid of this bad habit within two days. How fast! Hence, I highly recommend high-school and college students to give it a read. I guarantee that there will be a tramendous, postive change on you.

Book 4: 1984

1984 and Fahrenheit 451 share a lot of similarities as both of them are dystopian novels. However, not all of their contents are dystopian; more than three-quarter content in 1984 depicts a brutal, paralyzed world under totalitarian regimes — a ubiquitous phenomenon in World War II. George Orwell warned the contemporary society that every totalitarian country would likely end up being filled with censorship and ignorance as the world he described in the book, no matter it is communism or fascism. It is also a thought-provoking book, which made me assert that actions have to be taken in order for the oligarchy to come to an end. Otherwise, people will be totally controlled, confused by the world around them and end up having no ability to think logically, because they have to obey the dictators who presumptuously change the law or the constitutions whenever they want to. Any personal revolt will result in "disappearing" from this world.
