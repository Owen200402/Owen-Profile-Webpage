Introduction

Owen was born in Shanghai, China -- one of the busiest, prosperious metropolises worldwide. He moved to Canada at the age of 14 in grade 9. At that period, he struggled to make progress on his English. However, Owen has an indomitable spirit. He became brave to speak English with his friends. Eventually, he took the advantage of the environment he was living in to maximally elevate his English. Meanwhile, he seemed to find his career path through try-and-error. He became aware of the growth of technology industry. He first tried it out by taking Mosh Hamedani's ultimate java series. After he completed a third of it, he confidently shouted in front of his mom, "This is what I want!" He could sit in front of a computer and write code persistently for 3 hours. --"Writing a program that simulate a real world problem has so much fun!" Because of the passion, Owen digged into programming, kept his perserverance and attended competitions held by waterloo (Canadian Computing Competition) in which he scored decent marks.

Volunteer Experience

Owen has participated in a variety of volunteer activities in community. He has been involved and become one of the most popular tutors in his school's homework club. Students often feel satisfied from his tutoring. "Owen is very passionate about teaching and his approach of teaching made us easy to understand!" said by one of his formal tutees. He is extremely organized and willing to spend time preparing materials for his tutees, which is a trait that the majority of the other tutors do not have. Besides the homework club, he is also a vice president of Sunshine Foundation Piano Club. This club is a place where he organize events such as a virtual concert performance. He also helped raise funds for BC hospital donation, especially to kids who were in risks of death. During Covid, he also helped SDC (Social Diversity for Children Foundation) build classrooms for disabled students through an auction in which they collected 20k dollars. Furthermore, Owen also helped Quantum Academy edit video for their talent shows. They eventually received 100k dollars and donated all of them to BC hospital as well.

Life with Music

At young age, Owen showed his strong passion in music, being obsessed into classical music in particular. He officially started to learn piano when he was 4. Owen insisted on becoming a great musician and practiced piano regularly and assiduously. He has gained tons of district and provincial prizes when he was in the middle school. He also accomplished RCM level 10 practical exam quickly by spending 1 year practicing these designated music pieces and got an overall 90%. Last year December, Owen was stunned that he received the first place in an international competition -- Elite Music International Competition. His expectation for his future is never giving up playing music, catching every chance to improve his artistry even further. Moreover, he also thinks of music as a secondary major choice in University. Therefore, he is planning to join the live audition in 2021 in University of Mcgill. Wish him all the best.

Fahrenheit 451 The Kite Runner Atomic Habits

Book 1: Fahrenheit 451

Fahrenheit 451 is one of the most inspiring books I have ever read in my life, and it triggered my passion for reading. The book is based on a simulated world in which technology dominates the world; people always use their technology negatively by neglecting face-to-face conversation, sports and social lives. Also, it captures a society in which books are strictly banned under censorship. If you collect books at home, the police can give you the death penalty, because they think you have an unchaste, distorted mind that can be harmful to the technology-disoriented society. Interestingly, with the alignment of the police's job, firemen are asked to burn the books rather than put out fires. Overall, this dystopian book has been a great inspiration to lots of young readers, since the story is highly related to our day-to-day life and it seems likely to happen in our lives as the technology industry is skyrocketing. It gives everyone a warning to not fully depend on technology, which would eventually lead to the tragic ending described in the book.

Book 2: The Kite Runner

I enjoyed reading the kite runner. This is one of the best non-fiction books I have ever read. The story reflects the cruelty of the war in Afghanistan's history. However, author Hosseni chose to focus more on a family's relationship, which brought me into tears a lot of times. There are many heartbreaking and heartwarming scenes inside that have been foreshadowed and somewhat predictable. One of the most valuable thing to me by reading this novel is that I developed my ability to imagine while reading, which increased my interest of reading as well. I strongly recommend this book to high school students, because the story was vividly depicted and could trigger their nerve to read more non-fictions. Once you read it, you can hardly put it down.

Book 3: Atomic Habits

Atomic Habits -- An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones. As its name, this book can let a person be aware his good or bad habits by applying effective implementations. This is the book that could have taken me a month to borrow from the library because of the long waiting list in front. However, I finally decided to buy it from a local bookstore. Honestly, this is the book that shaped my life the most; I felt the power of a fiction book. This book is structured clearly with visual graphs every five to ten pages and a little summary near the end of each chapter. The author discussed four core concepts of building and destorying every habit -- cue, craving, respose, and reward. While I was reading the book, I also tried to discover my habits and improve. Surprisingly, by applying these principles, I was able to detect my weeknesses / bad habits in my life and change them back in an astonishing speed. For example, I used to use my bath rope to clean my mouth after brushing my teeth because it was close to me. My mom was always being mad at me because of this bad habit. By applying the principles in this book, I completely got rid of this bad habit within two days. How fast! Hence, I highly recommend high-school and college students to give it a read. I guarantee you that there will be a huge postive change on you.
